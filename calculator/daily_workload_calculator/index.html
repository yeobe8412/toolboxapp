What the result means

The headline figure summarizes the modeled daily workload outcome under the current assumptions. The supporting rows separate important components so you can check whether the result is operationally or financially plausible.

Treat this as a planning estimate. Actual daily workload outcomes can differ because of timing, fees, taxes, rounding, eligibility rules, market conditions, or data quality that the simplified model does not capture.

Begin with the prefilled scenario: Available work time = 8; Focused work = 4; Meetings and admin = 2; Unplanned buffer = 1. Record the result, then change Available work time while holding the other inputs constant.

The difference between the two outputs shows the sensitivity of daily workload to that assumption. Repeat with Focused work for a second comparison.

What does the Daily Workload Calculator show?

It converts the entered Available work time, Focused work, Meetings and admin, Unplanned buffer assumptions into an indicative daily workload result and a supporting breakdown.

How should I choose a value for Available work time?

Use a current, documented figure when available. For forecasts, test a conservative case alongside your expected value rather than relying on a single assumption.

Why does Focused work materially change the estimate?

Focused work is part of the model's scale or rate relationship. Even a modest adjustment can compound or flow through several displayed figures.

Can I use the result as a final decision?

No. Use it to screen scenarios and identify trade-offs, then confirm material time & productivity decisions with source records and qualified advice where appropriate.
